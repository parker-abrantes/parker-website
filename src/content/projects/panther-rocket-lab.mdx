## Role

**Project Manager** — coordinated a 25+ member multidisciplinary aerospace team across 4 subsystem leads (structures, propulsion, avionics, ground systems) through an 8-month development cycle and 30+ design reviews and integration meetings. Managed a ~$10,000 project budget including vendor sourcing, purchasing approvals, and schedule enforcement.

**Treasurer** — currently help manage financial operations for a 100+ member engineering organization with an annual operating budget of approximately $30,000. Responsibilities include purchasing workflow, budget tracking, and resource allocation across subsystems and events.

## NAR Level 1 Certification

Built and launched a high-power rocket on a J-class motor. Painted blue with a gold stripe — harder to spot against the sky than anticipated. Reached approximately 2,000 ft apogee, deployed parachute, and recovered after a 40-minute search through open field and brush at the Compound in Palm Bay. The faint beeper inside eventually gave it away.

The rocket is named Susan.

## What I Learned

A rocket doesn't succeed because one person has a good design. It succeeds when people, budgets, timelines, interfaces, documentation, manufacturing, testing, and trust align. That lesson has shaped how I think about engineering more broadly: the technical and the organizational are not separate problems.
